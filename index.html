The Origin

From Frisco, with fork in hand.

Two friends. One pantry. A dozen-plus test batches.

0protein per serving
0students blind-tested
0chose retail over us

Paarth and Advik have loved hummus since they were kids. Growing up in Frisco, Texas, it was a fixture of childhood — on shawarmas, scooped with celery, paired with just about anything in the fridge.

As they got older, they fell into the same loop as everyone else: the protein chase. More protein, every meal, every day. But being tall and lean, hitting a gram of protein per pound of bodyweight was genuinely hard — and between recruiting and the daily grind, good options were thin. They were also chasing something bigger: a passion worth turning into work, the kind where your passion becomes the job.

The moment came at Insomnia Cookies. Mid-conversation, they spotted a cookie with 10 grams of protein — and it clicked. They could build a protein alternative of their own. The market was already crowded with protein products, so they went somewhere no one had: a spread. A protein-packed condiment nobody thinks of as a protein source, with a thick, satisfying texture that's genuinely filling. Vegetarians struggle with that more than anyone, so they set out to build something you could pair with anything and actually feel full.

After plenty of trial and error, they landed on an all-natural, homemade recipe — then put it to the test. Blind-tasted against the leading store-bought hummus with 400 students at UT Austin, not a single person chose the retail jar. That was all the fire they needed to go revolutionize the hummus game.

"We didn't want to invent a new food. We wanted to perfect one we already loved." — Paarth & Advik

That's SUMMUH. Hummus, rewound — back to what it should've been all along.

What's In It

Three things we refused to compromise on.

Sum-thing Natural.

Whole-food ingredients. No whey isolates, no protein powders, no artificial anything. Just real food, blended right.

Sum-diddly Humption.

10g per serving — 59% more than traditional hummus. Built for vegetarians who lift, runners who snack, and anyone tracking macros without giving up flavor.

Sum-Too Creamy.

Unreasonably smooth — texture you'd expect from a Mediterranean café, not a grocery aisle. Never chalky, no protein-powder aftertaste.
